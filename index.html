About me

I am an ecological modeler interested in tropical forests, demography, and remote sensing. Currently, I am a last-year Ph.D. candidate at the Caughlin Lab looking for a postdoc. My dissertation is about predicting and understanding natural regeneration at landscape scales. We are linking novel high-resolution remote sensing products with field data using a Bayesian modeling approach to up-scale the natural regeneration demography to landscape scales.

In my free time, I like playing almost any kind of sport, including climbing and football. I also enjoy drawing, which I like to combine with my research to create sci-comm products.
